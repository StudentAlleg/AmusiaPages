Amusia

Amusia is a 2D Metroidvania game about Lambert, who stumbles into a fantasy world of classical music. His goal is to destroy this world by finding its cores and destroying them. Venture in this world as you explore musically inspired areas and landscapes while defending yourself against wild life and the world’s inhabitants. This game has fast paced and challenging combat inspired by Sekiro. Explore the world using fluid and momentous movement inspired by Celeste. Experience satisfying geographical and gameplay progression inspired by Hollow Knight. And most importantly, uncover Lambert’s story.

Constraints

This is a vertical slice made for CMPM-171 at UCSC. We had to operate under the following constraints:

Accessibility

Game must be Completable by the people with the following:

  • Limited Vision (Seeing)
  • Limited Dexterity (Touching)
  • Limited Audition (Hearing)
  • Limited Energy and Attention (Resting)

Amusia allows control rebinding, which allows for players to play with any device they prefer. Amusia is scaled so that one-handed players could beat it. Colors are not important to gameplay, in grayscale, all required features are distinct enough to work. Sound is used as an added benefit to gameplay designed, but is not required to complete the game.

Localization

  • Your game must support players two use two different written languages. At least one language must use either a logographic script or a right-to-left script.
  • The ability to localize them game to additional written languages should be supported by your localization kit.

On loading the game, there is a language select screen. In this screen, you can choose between US English and Simplified Chinese. This can also be accessed through settings. In our Unity project, we have localization tables, which can be used to add more languages to the game in the future.

Game Format

  • The game must be entirely completable while the player's device is disconnected from the internet.
  • The total data transfer involved in downloading the game must be less than 100 MiB Links to an external site..
  • The game may target the platform of the team's choice (e.g. desktop, mobile, console, browser, etc.). However, if Windows is supported macOS must also be supported (at least in principle). If iOS is supported, Android must be supported too (at least in principle).

Pre CMPM 171
